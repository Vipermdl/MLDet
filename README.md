## Abstract
A significant amount of marine litter has accumulated at sea surface, on the sea floor, and in coastal beaches all over the world due to the slow rate of breakdown of marine litter and the constant increase in manufacturing of single-use plastic goods in the past five decades. Robust and effective detection algorithm would therefore be valuable for recognizing and removing marine litter. But despite prior studies of effort, detecting litter object in real-world marine environment remains problematic. Here, we provide an efficient and accurate deep learning method to deal with marine litter detection named MLDet. All modules and techniques in MLDet are simple to implement built on pre-existing modules. The proposed framework has been verified on the public TrashCan dataset and the experimental results demonstrate that by using several tricks the detection performance has been significantly improved as compared to the generic object detectors. More importantly, MLDet achieves state-of the-art performance with adding small amount of computational costs.
